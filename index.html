Survivorship Curves

About

1. The dynamic content demonstrates types of survivorship curves.
2. The learner can observe and learn the distribution of different animals in three survivorship curves.
3. This activity will help learners to understand and retain the knowledge of the distribution of animals according to their survivorship curve.

Instruction

1. Learner can see the introduction and image of types of survivorship curves.
2. Learners can click on the flashcards with the name of types of survivorship curves.
3. Once learner click on the specific card, they can see the detailed graph, information, and images of different animals of that type of curve.
4. Learners can swipe the flashcards to know about different types of curves.

A survivorship curve is the graphical presentation of the number of individuals in a population that can be anticipated to survive to any specific age. There are three important types of survivorship curves Type I, Type II, and Type III. By observing these survivorship curves we can understand the maximum life expectancy of different types of organisms.
